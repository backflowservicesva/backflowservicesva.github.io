Frequently Asked Questions

What is a backflow preventer?

It is a mechanical device installed on your water system that prevents contaminated water from flowing backward into the clean public water supply.

Why do I need a test every year?

Backflow valves have internal seals, springs, and moving parts that degrade over time. Annual testing is mandated by the state of Virginia and local counties to ensure the device is still functioning.

Do I need to be present for the test?

In most cases, if the backflow device is located outside, you do not need to be home. We will test the device, leave a copy of the results, and submit the official report to the county.
